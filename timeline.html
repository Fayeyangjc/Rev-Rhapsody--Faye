Timeline: From Karting to F1

Ages 6-10 – Karting

- Young drivers start in local karting competitions, learning basic racing skills.

- Compete in national and international karting championships (e.g., CIK-FIA).

Ages 11-15 – Advanced Karting & Junior Formula

- Move up to higher-level karting competitions (e.g., European Championships).

- Some transition into entry-level single-seater racing (e.g., Formula 4).

Ages 15-18 – Formula 4 to Formula 3

- Join Formula 4 (F4) in national series (e.g., Italian F4, British F4).

- Progress to Formula 3 (F3), racing in international series.

Ages 18-21 – Formula 2 (F2) & F1 Testing

- Compete in Formula 2 (F2), the final step before F1.

- Get F1 test opportunities and enter driver academies.

Ages 20-25 – Formula 1 Debut

- Earn an FIA Super License.

- Join an F1 team as a reserve driver or secure a race seat.

- Begin F1 career, aiming for podiums and championships.
